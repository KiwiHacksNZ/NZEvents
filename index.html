The two-way problem

Providers

External organisations want to reach students directly but get gatekept by school admin. Great opportunities die in teacher inboxes.

Teachers

Broadcasting isn't part of their job and they get no reward for it. Even passionate teachers burn out being the middleman.

The Fix

A student-led platform that teachers can point to instead of forwarding emails. Everyone wins — students find opportunities, teachers offload, providers reach their audience.

Built by students, for students

Your school experience shouldn't depend on whether your teacher forwards an email. NZ Events exists because too many students miss opportunities — not because of budget, not because of ability — just because nobody told them.

We're a student-led initiative collecting and sharing every competition, scholarship, workshop, and opportunity we can find. If it's open to NZ students, it belongs here.

Want to add an event?

This site is open source. Fork the repo, add your event to events.json, and open a pull request.

1

Fork the repo

Head to GitHub and fork kiwihacksnz/nzevents to your account.

2

Edit events.json

Add your event as a new entry. Follow the existing format — name, org, category, region, date, years, description, link, and urgent.

3

Open a PR

Submit a pull request back to the main repo. We'll review it and merge it in.

Example entry

{
  "name": "Your Event Name",
  "org": "Organisation",
  "category": "competition",
  "region": "auckland",
  "date": "2026-06-01",
  "years": "Year 9-13",
  "description": "What it is and why students should care.",
  "link": "https://example.com",
  "contact": "hello@example.com",
  "urgent": false
}
